Case Studies

Real examples of HR work made more visible, repeatable, and human.

Short, anonymized stories showing how I think through HR operations problems.

Employee Connection

Keeping HR present across three continents with Slack and Zapier.

Context

I support employees spread across NAM, LATAM, and Europe. Scheduled check-ins were useful, but the time between meetings could create silence and make HR feel farther away than I wanted.

Approach

I used Slack IDs and Zapier to create a lightweight check-in workflow based on when I last connected with each employee. After about 20 days, the workflow sends a friendly, varied message.

Outcome

Employees respond, HR stays visible in recent chats, and people are more likely to reach out quickly. The automation supports connection; it does not replace my regular calls.

Documentation

Turning scattered process knowledge into SOPs.

Context

HR process knowledge often lives in notes, repeated questions, emails, and memory. That makes work harder to repeat, audit, or hand off.

Approach

I organize rough information into purpose, owner, trigger, steps, exceptions, templates, escalation paths, and follow-up rhythm. AI can help structure a first draft, but HR reviews the final output.

Outcome

Clearer documentation gives employees, managers, and HR a shared reference point. It reduces repeated clarification and makes the process easier to maintain.

Visibility

Using dashboards to reduce follow-up noise.

Context

Open HR items can become hard to track when they span regions, vendors, employees, managers, and legal or internal stakeholders.

Approach

I build trackers and reporting views that clarify owner, status, next step, region, and timeline. The goal is not more reporting for its own sake; it is shared visibility.

Outcome

When the work is visible, follow-up becomes easier, handoffs are cleaner, and HR can act more proactively instead of relying on memory or scattered updates.
