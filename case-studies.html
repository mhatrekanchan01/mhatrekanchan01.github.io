Case Study 1

Slack + Zapier HR Check-in Workflow

Problem

Employee check-ins across distributed teams can easily become inconsistent when they depend only on manual reminders, calendar discipline, and scattered notes. I wanted a lightweight way to create a repeatable check-in rhythm without turning it into another heavy HR process.

What I Built

I built a Slack + Zapier workflow to automate check-in nudges, capture responses, and create better visibility into follow-ups. The workflow helped standardize how employee touchpoints were initiated and tracked, while still keeping the interaction simple for employees.

Tools / Methods

  • Slack
  • Zapier
  • Google Sheets / tracker-based workflow
  • Reminder logic
  • Response tracking
  • HR process design

Outcome

The workflow reduced manual chasing, made follow-ups easier to track, and created a more consistent rhythm for employee connects across a distributed team.

What This Shows

This project shows my ability to identify repeated HR admin work and convert it into a simple automation that improves consistency without over-engineering the process.

Case Study 3

Meal Stipend Budget Analysis

Problem

Employees were frequently paying out of pocket for office meal orders, but the issue needed data before recommending a budget change. The question was whether the existing stipend was actually too low or whether the concern was based on a few isolated cases.

What I Built

I analyzed three months of DoorDash office meal order data across 82 orders. I compared the current $20 budget with a proposed $25 budget and calculated employee out-of-pocket impact, average top-up, fully covered orders, and estimated annual company cost.

Key Numbers

  • 82 orders reviewed over 3 months
  • Average order value: $25.02
  • 73 out of 82 orders, or 89%, were above the $20 budget
  • Average employee top-up: $5.25
  • Out-of-pocket amount would reduce from $430.46 to $155.06
  • Estimated incremental annual company cost: approximately $1,102

Tools / Methods

  • Spreadsheet analysis
  • Cost comparison
  • Scenario modeling
  • Employee experience lens
  • Decision-ready recommendation

Outcome

The analysis converted a small employee experience issue into a clear, data-backed recommendation. It showed the tradeoff between employee impact and incremental company cost.

What This Shows

This project shows my ability to use simple data analysis to support practical People Ops decisions.

Case Study 4

Multi-country Leave & Vendor Data Reconciliation

Problem

In multi-country HR operations, leave balances, EOR records, HRIS data, and vendor calculations can create confusion when they do not match. Employees and managers need clear answers, but HR first needs a reliable way to reconcile source-of-truth data.

What I Built

I created reconciliation views and explanation formats to compare HRIS records, vendor/EOR calculations, accruals, carryovers, expiries, and utilization. The work involved translating vendor logic into employee-friendly explanations and internal decision notes.

Tools / Methods

  • HRIS data review
  • Vendor data reconciliation
  • Leave balance calculation
  • Source-of-truth mapping
  • Employee communication drafts
  • Process documentation

Outcome

The work reduced ambiguity, helped create clearer responses, and improved confidence in how leave balances and vendor-driven calculations were explained.

What This Shows

This project shows my strength in handling messy HR operations details, structuring the facts, and turning them into clear process outputs.
